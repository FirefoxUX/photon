Palettes

Find details on each of our colors below, indicating color name, hex value, and contrast ratings for usage with text.

When using colors with text, always opt to have either white typography or white background. #ffffff Only use a combination, if it reaches an adequate color contrast, to ensure accessibility. This means it should have a rating of AA, or AAA in the table below. If your text is at least 18pt or set in bold with at least 14pt, a rating of AA Large is acceptable too.

Combinations of text on colorful background that fail the contrast rating, should use the darkest shade–90–of the same color for the typography. Visible on the contrast ratings below.

Accessibility

Color Contrast

Follow the WCAG 2.0 Level AA guidelines to ensure proper color contrast. Bodies of text should have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 and large-scale text should have a contrast ratio of at least 3:1.

Colour Contrast Analyser contrast and color blindness analyzer (Windows and macOS) by The Paciello Group.

Contrast Ratio contrast ratio calculator (Web) by Lea Verou.

Color Blind

There are different types of color blindness. The most common form is reg-green color blindness, followed by blue-yellow color blindness and total color blindness. Red-green color blindness affects up to 8% of males and 0.5% of females. Ensure that adjacent color shades are distinguishable for color blind people. In case of doubts use a color blindness analyzer.

Sim Daltonism color blindness simulator (iOS and macOS) by Michel Fortin.

High Contrast on Windows

Windows currently offers multiple high contrast modes. Ensure that colors are translated properly, following the hierarchy defined for the default theme.

Supporting High Contrast Themes on Windows guidelines by Windows Dev Center.
